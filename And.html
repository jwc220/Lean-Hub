And

The "And" rule in predicate logic allows you to deduce components of a conjunction:

  • Left And Elimination (∧ elimL): From a proof of α ∧ β, deduce a proof of α.

  • Right And Elimination (∧ elimR): From a proof of α ∧ β, deduce a proof of β.

  • And Introduction (∧ intro): Given proofs of α and β, produce a proof of α ∧ β.

These rules can be used to deconstruct conjunctions or combine individual propositions into a conjunction.

Example: Given h : (p ∧ q) ∧ r, derive q.

Proof: We have h2 : q ∧ r, from ∧ elimL on h. The result, to show q, follows from ∧ elimR on h2.
