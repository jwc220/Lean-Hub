Or

The "Or" rule in predicate logic includes Or Introduction and Or Elimination:

Or Introduction:

  • Left Or Introduction (∨ introL): From a proof of α, this gives a proof of α ∨ β.

  • Right Or Introduction (∨ introR): From a proof of β, this gives a proof of α ∨ β.

Or Elimination:

  • Given a proof of α ∨ β, a proof of γ on the assumption of α, and a proof of γ on the assumption of β, Or Elimination (∨ elim) produces a proof of γ.

These rules are applied to construct disjunctions or deduce propositions from disjunctions. Problems involve using these rules to derive propositions and identify errors in arguments.

Or Introduction:

Example: Given h : p, derive p ∨ q.

Proof: The result follows from ∨ introL on h.

Try in Lean 

example (p q : Prop) (h : p) : p ∨ q :=

begin

  from or.inl h,
 
end

Or Elimination:

Example: Given h : p ∨ q, derive q ∨ p.

In Lean: lemma or.swap (h : p ∨ q) : q ∨ p :=

Proof: Our task is to (1) assume p and derive q ∨ p and separately to (2) assume q and derive q ∨ p.

      1. Assume h2 : p. We show q∨ p from ∨introR on h2.

      2. Assume h3 : q. We show q∨ p from ∨introL on h3.

Try in Lean 

example (p q : Prop) (h : p ∨ q) : q ∨ p :=
    
begin
    
 have h₁ : p → (q ∨ p),
  { assume k : p,
    show q ∨ p, from or.inr k, },

  have h₂ : q → (q ∨ p),
  { assume k : q,
     show q ∨ p, from or.inl k, },

  show q ∨ p, from or.elim h h₁ h₂,

 end

Exercise: Prove (p ∧ q) ∨ (r ∧ p).

Try in Lean 
example (p q r : Prop) (h : (p ∧ q) ∨ (r ∧ p)) : p :=

...

Proof By Cases

    Case 1: Assume (p ∧ q) and use conjunction elimination to derive p.

    Case 2: Assume (r ∧ p) and use conjunction elimination to derive p.

In both cases, p is derived, showing that it follows from the given assumption (p ∧ q) ∨ (r ∧ p). 
begin
    
 have h₁ : (p ∧ q) → p,
  { assume k : p ∧ q,
    show p, from k.left, },

  have h₂ : (r ∧ p) → p,
  { assume k : r ∧ p,
     show p, from k.right, },

  show p, from or.elim h h₁ h₂,

 end
