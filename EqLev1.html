Implication

The "Implication" rule in predicate logic comprises:

  • Implication Elimination (→elim): Given proofs of α → β and α, allows deriving a proof of β.

  • Implication Introduction (→intro): To prove α → β, assume h: α and derive β under this assumption.

These rules facilitate the deduction of propositions based on implications and the establishment of implications through assumption and derivation.

Implication Elimination:

Example: Given h₁ : p → (q ∧ r) and h₂ : p, derive r.

In Lean: (h₁ : p → (q ∧ r)) (h₂ : p) : r :=

Proof: We have h₃ : q ∧ r from →elim h₁, h₂. The result follows from ∧ elimR h₃.

In Lean:

begin

have h₃ : q ∧ r, from h₁ h₂

show r, from h₃.right,

end
